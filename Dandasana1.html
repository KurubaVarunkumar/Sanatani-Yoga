Steps to do Dhandasana

  1. Seated Position: Sit on the floor with your legs extended straight in front of you.
  2. Legs and Feet: Keep your legs together and extended, with the feet flexed. Point your toes toward the ceiling.
  3. Spine Alignment: Maintain a tall and straight spine. Engage your core muscles to support the lengthening of the spine.
  4. Hand Placement: Place your palms on the floor beside your hips. Press your hands into the floor to lift your chest.
  5. Shoulder and Neck: Roll your shoulders back and down. Keep your chin parallel to the floor, and gaze straight ahead.

Benifits

  • Strengthens the muscles of the back and core.
  • Stretches the legs, particularly the hamstrings.
  • Improves posture and alignment.
  • Prepares the body for other seated and forward-bending poses.

    • Note

  • Individuals with lower back issues or certain injuries may need to modify the pose or avoid it.
