2.Kapalbhati Pranayama:

Kapalbhati Pranayama is a yogic breathing exercise that is part of the ancient Indian practice of Hatha Yoga. The name "Kapalbhati" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "kapal," meaning skull or forehead, and "bhati," meaning shining or illuminating. Therefore, Kapalbhati is often translated as "Skull Shining Breath" or "Shining Skull Breath."

how to do

  • Sitting Position: Sit comfortably in a cross-legged position on the floor or on a chair with an erect spine. Ensure that your shoulders are relaxed.
  • Hand Placement: Place your hands on your knees with palms facing upward. You can also use the Jnana Mudra, where you touch the tip of your index finger to the tip of your thumb, while keeping the other three fingers extended.
  • Focus on Breath:Take a few deep breaths to relax and center yourself. Kapalbhati involves short, powerful exhales, emphasizing the active exhalation while keeping the inhalation passive.
  • Kapalbhati Technique:Inhale comfortably and then forcefully exhale through the nose by contracting the abdominal muscles. The emphasis is on the exhalation, and the inhalation should happen naturally as a result of releasing the abdominal contraction. The exhale should be active, quick, and rhythmic, with a short, sharp burst of breath. The inhalation is more passive and occurs naturally as the abdomen relaxes.
  • Repetitions: Start with a few rounds of Kapalbhati, gradually increasing the pace and duration as your comfort level and proficiency improve. A typical session may consist of 50 to 100 rapid breaths, followed by a deep inhalation and exhalation.
  • Ending the Practice: After completing the rounds, take a few deep breaths and allow your breath to return to its natural rhythm. Observe any sensations in your body.
