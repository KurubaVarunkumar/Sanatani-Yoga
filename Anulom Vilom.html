5. Nadi Shodhana Variation

Anulom Vilom is a pranayama technique also known as "Alternate Nostril Breathing." It is a popular and versatile breathing exercise in yoga that helps balance the flow of energy in the body, enhance respiratory function, and bring about a sense of calm and balance. Anulom Vilom is similar to Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing) but has some variations in the technique.

how to do

  • Sitting Position: Sit comfortably in a cross-legged position on the floor or on a chair with an erect spine. Ensure that your shoulders are relaxed.
  • Hand Position (Mudra): Use the right thumb to close off the right nostril and the right ring finger or pinky finger to close off the left nostril. The other fingers can rest gently on your forehead..
  • Anulom Vilom Technique: Start by closing your right nostril with the right thumb and inhaling slowly and deeply through the left nostril. After the inhalation, close the left nostril with the right ring finger or pinky finger, release the right nostril, and exhale slowly and completely through the right nostril. Inhale again through the right nostril, then close it, release the left nostril, and exhale through the left nostril. This completes one cycle. Continue alternating nostrils for several rounds.
  • Smooth and Controlled Breathing: Focus on making the breath smooth, controlled, and without any force. The inhalation and exhalation should be of equal duration.
  • As you become comfortable with the practice, you can gradually increase the duration of inhalation, exhalation, and the retention between breaths.
  • Ending the Practice: After completing several rounds, finish with an exhalation through the left nostril. Release the hand mudra and allow the breath to return to its natural rhythm.
